How TRIBE Decoder Works
No LLM generates interpretations. Every score cites a published paper. Every activation is predicted by TRIBE v2, trained on 500+ hours of real fMRI data.
THE PIPELINE — 6 STEPS
1
TEXT → SPEECH → WORD-LEVEL TIMINGTRIBE v2 uses a text-to-speech engine internally to synthesize audio from your input. WhisperX then performs forced-alignment to assign every word to a specific time offset. This is why inference takes 30–120 seconds — the speech synthesis and alignment steps run on every query.
2
MULTI-MODAL FEATURE EXTRACTIONTwo parallel encoders process the content: a LLaMA 3.2-3B language model extracts semantic and contextual features from text, while a Wav2Vec-BERT model extracts acoustic/prosodic features from the synthesized audio. Both streams are combined.
3
BOLD SIGNAL PREDICTION (20,484 VERTICES)The TRIBE v2 transformer (trained on 500+ hours of naturalistic fMRI data from 700+ subjects) predicts a BOLD fMRI time series across all 20,484 vertices of the fsaverage5 cortical surface mesh. Output shape: (n_seconds, 20,484). This represents where the brain would activate when hearing this content.
4
SCHAEFER 2018 ATLAS MAPPINGThe 20,484 vertex values are averaged within each of the 200 named parcels of the Schaefer 2018 atlas. Each parcel gets a single mean activation value. These are then z-scored relative to each other, so z=0 is average activation for this content, z=2 means 2 standard deviations above average.
5
YEO 7-NETWORK AGGREGATIONThe 200 parcel z-scores are grouped into 7 functional networks following the Yeo 2011 parcellation: Default Mode (DMN), Limbic (LMB), Salience/VentAttn (SAL), Dorsal Attention (DAN), Frontoparietal Control (FPN — the analytical/protective network), Somatomotor (SMN), and Visual (VIS).
6
RULE-BASED REACTION PROFILE SCORINGSix emotional reaction profiles are scored from the network activation pattern using weighted combinations of network z-scores. Every scoring rule cites a specific published neuroscience paper. No LLM generates the scores or text. The manipulation index = top emotional profile score × 5 + analytical bypass score × 5, capped at 10.
KNOWN LIMITATIONS
LIMBIC ALWAYS NEAR ZEROThe Schaefer 200-parcel atlas has limited limbic coverage compared to dedicated amygdala/hippocampus atlases. Emotional content may show lower limbic scores than expected. This is a known atlas coverage issue, not a model failure.
SHORT TEXT (<8 TRs) = UNRELIABLE SIGNALBelow 6 seconds of content the activation estimates are based on very few timepoints. Results are directionally informative but should not be interpreted confidently.
POPULATION AVERAGE — NOT INDIVIDUALTRIBE v2 predicts the average neural response across the training population. Individual responses can differ substantially based on prior beliefs, expertise, and emotional state.
ACTIVATION ≠ MANIPULATION INTENTHigh emotional activation scores mean the content predicted strong emotional brain circuit activation. This does not imply the creator intended manipulation; emotionally evocative language appears in poetry, literature, and public health communication too.
